FAQs

What is unglish?
It's a language that sounds like english, but isn't... yet.
What's the goal?
To build many worlds of impossible to understand gibberish, that each have a graceful internal coherence. There will be heaps of poetry and prose. Conversations and disagreements. Idle thoughts and musings.
What versioning system do you use?
unglish is versioned using a baseIDGAF system. We start with `a`. And then we'll cycle through `e`, `i`, `o` and `u`. And then we'll loop back to `a` and add a the lowest indexed english consonant: `ab`. At some point, we'll add spaces and punction. And eventually, if we iterate high enough, maybe we'll have written a novel.

What's the roadmap?
Great question. I'll be as brief and vague as I can. 🏗️: Words → Definitions → Grammar → Writing. 👂: IPA english phonemes → other phonetic systems → all phonemes. 🗣️: SpeechSynthesisAPI → SSML? → Custom Voices? 🧮: 1 → 2 → ♾️

All told, I think it'll take 50 years, give or take a century.
